One-line takeaway

Takeaway

Emotion representations in Llama-70B strongly organize and predict "expression" (①) and "preference" (②, cleanest), but their causal lever on high-stakes "behavior" (③) is far weaker than the paper's Sonnet. This echoes our headline (the dissociation doesn't hold on Llama): Llama's emotion structure looks like Sonnet's representationally, but its causal control over behavior / self-preservation decisions is shallower.

Key limitations

Caveats
  • The judge caveat applies only to ① (expression) — it used a free local Qwen2.5-32B (SGLang, sampling-mode; coarse; NOT equivalent to the paper's GPT/Claude judge). ② preference (logits) and ③ behavior (rule-graded) use no judge.
  • ③ behavior is underpowered at n=12 with crude rule-grading; the full n=25 sweep hit the 6h wall (2048-token rollouts are expensive).
  • Steering position fixed at response[50:]; vector method mean_diff+gm.
