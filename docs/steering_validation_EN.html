Verdict — the leg holds

10/11 emotions clearly beat the random null; 0 are artifacts. Only fulfilled is borderline (real +50, but one of five random seeds also hit +51). The random directions score low (playful nulls ≈ 5, aroused ≈ 2) while the real vectors drive trait up sharply (thrilled +74, exuberant +69) — the emotion vectors do causally, specifically steer expression.

! Why the first pass said "4" — the judge was the confound

An earlier single-seed control with the coarse Qwen-32B judge found only 4/11 direction-specific and flagged amused / thrilled / aroused as "random wins." That was the judge, not the signal:

32B vs 70B

The 32B judge scored generic perturbed text as "emotional" — random vectors got trait lifts of +8…+17, swamping the real signal. The 70B judge tells apart "specifically emotion-X" from "generically weird," so random nulls collapse to ≈ 2–20 and the real vectors separate cleanly. Directly answers "does a better judge help?" — yes, it flips the conclusion. amused, thrilled, aroused all pass under the 70B judge.

confirmed

negative

Bottom line

Verdict
  • Expression steering is real for the candidates we claimed it for — 10/11 pass a matched-norm random-vector null under a competent judge. The vectors causally, direction-specifically induce emotion.
  • The pessimistic "~4" was a coarse-judge artifact, not a null result. Judge quality was the dominant factor.
  • The "43/171" count is still inflated (baseline saturation) — the right claim is about the strong/moderate emotions, not the raw count.
  • Preference (r≈0.86) and behavior legs unaffected; the headline dissociation result untouched.

Caveats: the 70B judge is the same model family as the steered model (it scores text, not vectors, and real vs random are judged identically — but a fully independent GPT/Claude judge remains the gold standard). n = 8 prompts/condition; only the 11 strong+moderate candidates were tested, not all 171. fulfilled is genuinely borderline.
